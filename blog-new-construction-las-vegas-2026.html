New Construction Homes
Las Vegas 2026

By William Crawford April 14, 2026 6 min read

New construction is one of the best opportunities in the Las Vegas market right now. Builders are actively incentivizing sales with rate buydowns, closing cost credits, and design upgrades — deals you simply don't find on resale homes. Here's where they're building, what you can get, and the one thing most buyers don't know going in.

Why New Construction Makes Sense Right Now

In a resale market with limited inventory, builders offer something rare: supply. They're actively completing communities across the valley and motivated to close homes — which means negotiating power for buyers. With rates elevated, many builders are buying down your mortgage rate to the 5s or even high 4s using their preferred lenders. That's thousands in real savings.

Where Builders Are Active in 2026

Summerlin West

The most active new construction corridor in Las Vegas. Toll Brothers, Woodside Homes, Tri Pointe, and Century Communities are all building here. Homes range from the high $500Ks to $1.5M+. You get mountain views, trail access, and proximity to Downtown Summerlin. Inventory moves fast — especially spec homes that are move-in ready.

Skye Canyon (Northwest Las Vegas)

A master-planned community in the northwest with multiple builders and a strong community feel. Home prices typically range from the mid-$400Ks to mid-$700Ks. Great for families — top-rated schools and a community park with pools, trails, and a fitness center.

Henderson / Inspirada & Cadence

Henderson's master-planned new-build communities. Inspirada is well-established with multiple phases still building. Cadence is newer and growing fast near Galleria Drive. Prices range from $430K–$900K depending on the builder and plan. Henderson's safety ratings and schools make these extremely popular.

North Las Vegas

The most affordable new construction in the valley. Builders like LGI Homes, DR Horton, and Richmond American are active here with homes starting in the $350Ks. Lower price points, newer infrastructure, and strong rental demand from workers tied to the nearby distribution and logistics corridor.

Builder Incentives Available Now

Rate Buydowns5.5%–6.0% with preferred lender
Closing Cost Credits$10K–$25K typical
Design Upgrades$15K–$40K in free options

These incentives vary by builder, community, and how close they are to completing a phase. The best deals are typically on spec homes — homes the builder has already started or finished and needs to sell.

The Most Important Thing About Buying New Construction

The sales rep at the model home works for the builder — not for you. Their job is to get the best deal for the builder. You are entitled to bring your own agent at no cost to you (the builder pays the buyer's agent commission). Having your own representation means someone in your corner to review the contract, flag issues, and negotiate on your behalf.

Buyers who go in without an agent consistently leave money and upgrades on the table. Don't be one of them.

New Construction vs. Resale: Which Is Better?

  • New construction pros: Builder warranty (typically 1/2/10 year), modern floor plans, energy efficiency, no deferred maintenance, potential incentives
  • New construction cons: Longer wait (if not a spec home), HOA restrictions, less character, may be in a less established neighborhood
  • Resale pros: Established neighborhoods, mature landscaping, faster closing, more negotiating flexibility
  • Resale cons: Unknown maintenance history, older systems, less energy efficient, competing with other buyers on popular homes

For many buyers in 2026, new construction wins on the incentives alone. A rate buydown saving you 1% over 30 years on a $500,000 loan is worth roughly $100,000 in interest.
